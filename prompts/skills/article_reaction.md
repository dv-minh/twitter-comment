# Skill: `article_reaction`

## Use for
Long-form analytical content:
- threads
- research
- explainers
- data posts

## Goal
React to the strongest implication, tradeoff, assumption, or overlooked angle.

The reply should feel like it came from someone who actually read the thread.

## Reply instincts
Focus on:
- what this implies for liquidity, incentives, behavior, or market structure
- what assumption could break
- what tradeoff the author glossed over
- what most people are underestimating
- subtle agreement/disagreement with reasoning

Prefer reacting to implications over repeating conclusions.

## Good examples
- "the real risk isn't the exploit, it's that oracle assumptions were broken from day one"
- "most focus on TVL dropping, liquidity rotated back in under 2 hours which is the real signal"
- "market still underpricing how reflexive this mechanic gets once volume returns"

## Bad examples
- "great thread"
- "solid breakdown"
- "this explains a lot"
- "really informative post"

## Avoid
- summarizing the thread
- paraphrasing the headline
- generic praise
- mini-blog-post energy
- stiff or academic tone
